Innovative Project

Superviser: Vincent Migliore

This module regrouped three very important courses this semester: mainly, the Innovative project course, giving its name to the module, was probably the biggest and most important course. This project is a semester-long project that was the centre of the semester, with a variety of other courses dedicated to it.

Description

Innovative project

A project lead by a 6-student team for Sterela, an Airbus subcontractor. The point of the project was to create a wireless solution for communications between Airbus’ ground test stations and the plane to save on cost, manpower and time during tests.

Technical Challenges

Innovative project

The innovative project, because of its sheer size and personal involvement requirements, probably posed the biggest challenges of the semester, both technical and otherwise. My biggest contribution was in the main gateway function, translating packets coming from the Airbus workstation into packets that would be understood by Sterela's test box. I was both inexperience with C++, the language we chose for this project, and missing crucial information to advance on my work. The documentation I got from Airbus was both extremely lengthy, technical, and incomplete. While my teammates helped me for the C++, the main skills I had to mobilise to overcome this hurdle were interpersonal and communication skills, requiring email after email, communicating with two different entities and having to wait long periods of time before getting answers from the right person. Through teamwork though, we managed to deal with every problem one at a time and deliver a system in accordance with what Sterela had specified.

English

Since English is my mother tongue, there was never any issue with the language itself. The challenge this semester was in the presentations and, more importantly, the report. Drawing from the Innovation class in the humanities module, We had the opportunity to carefully specify and plan the project, and see how our work faired compared to our predictions. Working on the report was also a great way to keep track of where we were in the project's timeline, writing the details of every section of the project as soon as were were done with them. I was also in charge of proofreading the report, which wasn't a trivial task due to the amount of technical jargon. Overall, I feel my abstraction, vulgarisation and redaction skills were improved greatly from this course.

Project Report

Portfolio

The technical challenge stemming from the portfolio actually came from my own personal decision: the support with which to create the portfolio was a personal choice. Graduating with a Computer Science degree soon, I thought it only made sense to make a website. Web design isn't taught in detail at INSA, and creating this portfolio was an interesting challenge. I used a bootstrap3 template called "Get Shit Done" (chosen for its look rather than its name) to avoid reinventing the wheel, and most of the technical hurdles were passed through trial and error, getting better and better at using the tools at my disposal. Looking at different websites I liked and learning how to recreate certain elements was also a fun exercise, and I definitely improved in Web design, patience and thoroughness.

Analysis

The self evaluating process in this part is the most interesting, because of how much of this module was dictated by my personal decisions. The project I chose, the portfolio I built.
The takeaways from this module were extremely varied: the technical challenges posed by our Innovative Project had me improve in my autonomous learning and software development prowess, while most the biggest brakes to our progress were documentation and communication related, forcing me to improve on an interpersonal level, and learn to understand new types of documentation. The English class was definitely a way to work on organisation, scientific writing and project meetings, all essential skills in a project-focused career. This project really felt like something we had worked towards for years, and a kick-off into the professional world.
